Our Hospital Services

In hospitals, audio-visual (AV) systems play a crucial role in improving patient care, facilitating communication, enhancing educational opportunities, and providing efficient administrative support. These systems are integrated into various aspects of hospital operations, from patient rooms to conference rooms, training spaces, and public areas. Patient Room Entertainment and Communication

Television and Streaming Services
Many hospitals provide in-room entertainment through TVs, offering patients access to movies, TV shows, and streaming services to help pass the time during their recovery.


Read More

Patient Education
AV systems are used to provide patients with educational content about their conditions, treatment plans, and recovery exercises. Interactive TVs or touchscreens in rooms allow patients to access personalized educational videos and information about their care.


Telemedicine & Remote Consultations
Video conferencing systems enable virtual consultations with doctors or specialists. This is especially useful for remote consultations, follow-ups, or connecting patients with specialists in different locations.


Voice-Controlled Systems
In some modern hospitals, smart devices (e.g., Alexa or Google Assistant) are used in patient rooms to control lights, temperature, TV, and other features hands-free.

Medical Training and Education

Simulation Labs
AV systems in medical simulation centers help train students and staff using HD cameras, microphones, and real-time recording for safe, hands-on procedure practice.


Read More

Medical Lectures & Webinars
Hospitals often host educational webinars, video conferences, or remote lectures for medical staff. AV systems (video conferencing tools like Zoom, Microsoft Teams, or WebEx) are used for these virtual continuing education sessions.


Video Recording for Teaching
Video cameras and microphones are used to record surgeries or medical procedures for educational purposes, allowing medical professionals to review and learn from real-life cases.

Conference and Meeting Rooms

Video Conferencing
Essential for hospital meetings, remote specialist consultations, and multi-campus communications using platforms like Zoom Rooms or Microsoft Teams.


Read More

Presentation Equipment
Includes projectors, large screens, and digital displays for presenting medical data, research, and live patient data during reviews, integrated with hospital systems.


Microphones and Audio Systems
Wireless microphones and sound systems ensure clear audio for all participants, even in larger conference rooms.

Patient Monitoring and Alerts

Visual and Audio Alerts
AV systems notify staff with alarms and updates when urgent care is needed.

Patient Monitoring Systems
Real-time displays show vital signs like heart rate and oxygen levels for ICU patients.

Visitor Communication

Video Calling
Patients connect with family and friends through video calls when in-person visits are restricted.

Digital Signage

Wayfinding Systems
Digital kiosks and displays guide patients and visitors around the facility.

Information Display
Screens share hospital news, wait times, and health tips in public areas.

Surgical and Operating Rooms

Surgical Imaging Systems
HD cameras and monitors display patient vitals and surgery views for the medical team.

Real-Time Streaming
Surgeries are streamed for educational purposes, enabling live learning.


Collaboration Tools

Relaxation Areas

Lounge Entertainment
